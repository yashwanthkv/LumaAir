The Solution

A modular exhaust-mounted node that treats a portion of the airstream in a bypass loop, minimizing pressure drop while enabling sorbent contact, PM filtration, and instrumented measurement. Data flows into a compliance-ready dashboard.

CO₂ Capture Node

Commercial amine/amine-silica (v1) with low-temp regeneration; CNF-amine (v2) for bio-based performance.

PM Filtration

Removes PM2.5/PM10 to improve local air quality and reduce visible plumes.

AI + Reporting

Sensors (CO₂/PM/flow/temp) with AI insights and automated reporting aligned to LL97/BERDO/BPS.

How it works

01 • Install

Clamp-on bypass module added to exhaust duct; no major HVAC changes.

02 • Capture

A fraction of flow passes through the sorbent cartridge for CO₂ capture + PM filtration.

03 • Regenerate

Low-temp regeneration (80–100 °C) on a duty cycle; cartridges are serviceable.

04 • Report

Real-time monitoring, MRV, avoided-emissions estimates, and automated compliance reports.

FAQ

Does this affect HVAC performance?

The node uses a bypass design to minimize pressure drop. Final sizing is confirmed during site assessment.

How is CO₂ capture measured?

Sensors (CO₂/flow/temp) plus duty cycles are used to estimate captured mass; data exports support audits.

What about maintenance?

Cartridges are designed for periodic swap and regeneration. Service schedules depend on load and operating hours.
